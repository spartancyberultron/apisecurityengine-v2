OWASP LLM10 addresses the risk of Model Theft, where unauthorized access and theft of a Large Language Model (LLM) by malicious actors can lead to significant security issues. This analysis uses the STRIDE and DREAD models to assess the severity of this threat.

STRIDE

  • Spoofing: (Low) An attacker is unlikely to spoof an LLM model itself.
  • Tampering: (Medium) An attacker might tamper with the model during theft, potentially corrupting its functionality.
  • Repudiation: (Low) Not a major concern for model theft.
  • Information Disclosure: (Critical) Exposing the model itself leaks valuable intellectual property and insights.
  • Denial-of-Service: (Medium) Stealing the model might limit access for legitimate users, though it does not constitute a complete denial of service.
  • Elevation of Privilege: (Low) Not applicable directly to model theft.

DREAD

  • Damage: (Critical) A stolen model can cause significant financial loss and reputational damage.
  • Reproducibility: (High) Once stolen, the model's capabilities can be easily replicated by the attacker.
  • Exploitability: (Medium) Requires some technical knowledge to exploit the stolen model effectively.
  • Affected Users: (High) Theft can impact all users relying on the LLM model, potentially leading to widespread issues.
  • Discoverability: (Medium) Model theft might not be immediately discovered, especially if the theft is discreet.

Risk Levels

Based on the STRIDE and DREAD analysis, the risk levels for OWASP LLM10: Model Theft are categorized as follows:

  • Critical: Information Disclosure (STRIDE), Damage (DREAD)
  • High: Reproducibility (DREAD), Affected Users (DREAD)
  • Medium: Tampering (STRIDE), Denial-of-Service (STRIDE), Exploitability (DREAD), Discoverability (DREAD)
  • Low: Spoofing (STRIDE), Repudiation (STRIDE), Elevation of Privilege (STRIDE)

Model Theft presents a high-risk vulnerability due to its potential for significant damage and impact on users. Implementing robust security measures and access controls is essential to mitigate this risk.
