OWASP LLM07 addresses the risks associated with insecure plugin design for Large Language Models (LLMs). Poorly designed plugins can introduce significant vulnerabilities. Let’s break down this risk using the STRIDE and DREAD threat modeling approaches.

Here’s a breakdown of the STRIDE and DREAD analysis:

STRIDE

  • Spoofing: (Low) An attacker might struggle to spoof a plugin due to potential authentication mechanisms.
  • Tampering: (Medium) Input manipulation through prompts or data fed to plugins could lead to unintended outputs.
  • Repudiation: (Low) Not a major concern for LLM07.
  • Information Disclosure: (High) Insecure plugins might leak sensitive information or LLM internals through outputs.
  • Denial-of-Service (DoS): (Medium) Malicious plugins could overload the LLM or consume resources excessively.
  • Elevation of Privilege: (Critical) A vulnerable plugin could allow attackers to gain unauthorized control over the LLM or underlying systems.

DREAD

  • Damage: (High) Data breaches, system disruptions, or manipulation of LLM outputs can cause significant damage.
  • Reproducibility: (Medium) Exploiting plugin vulnerabilities might require some effort but could be automated with some knowledge.
  • Exploitability: (High) Once a vulnerability is identified, exploiting it can be relatively simple.
  • Affected Users: (High) If the LLM is widely used, a successful attack can impact a large number of users.
  • Discoverability: (Medium) The complexity of discovering vulnerabilities depends on the plugin design and access.

Risk Categories

  • Critical: Elevation of Privilege vulnerabilities in plugins pose a critical risk.
  • High: Information disclosure from insecure plugins is a high risk.
  • Medium: DoS attacks and unintended outputs due to tampering with plugin inputs are medium risks.
  • Low: Spoofing a plugin is a low risk due to potential security measures.
